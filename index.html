Background

High performance liquid chromatography is ubiquitous in the pharmaceutical field. It separates substances in a sample and yields concentration or purity information. There's a plethora of applications in fields outside of pharma as well, like the food industry, urine analysis of drugs, and geology. I wanted to compile a few equations that have been useful to me regarding aspects of HPLC, and figured it might be interesting to implement calculators for each equation as well.

HPLC Column Volume

Inside an HPLC column during use, there is stationary phase and mobile phase. The stationary phase are the molecules bound to the inside of the column that provides it with its unique column chemistry (for example, a C8 or C18 or phenyl-hexyl column). The mobile phase is the solution that is flowing through the column. Column volume essentially refers to the volume of mobile phase can be contained in the column, both the volume outside of the packing particles, and the volume inside the pores of the packing particles, but not the packing particles themselves (i.e., the stationary phase).

Calculating the column volume is useful for choosing a run time for your HPLC method, selecting a gradient profile, and calculating solvent consumption requirements.
